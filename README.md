## The Problem

macOS Natural Scrolling works great for trackpads — but the moment you plug in an external mouse, the scroll wheel feels wrong. Enable Natural Scrolling and your trackpad feels right but your mouse scrolls backwards. Disable it and your mouse feels right but your trackpad scrolls backwards. There's no built-in way to fix both at once.

On top of that, third-party mice don't get the smooth momentum scrolling that trackpads do — every scroll notch feels abrupt and mechanical.

## The Fix

Mac Scroll Fix sits in your menu bar and intercepts only mouse scroll events — leaving your trackpad completely untouched. It applies smooth momentum and inertia to your mouse wheel, so your cheap Amazon mouse feels as natural as a Magic Trackpad.

- ✅ Trackpad keeps working exactly as before
- ✅ Mouse gets smooth, momentum-based scrolling
- ✅ No need to change any macOS system settings
- ✅ Works with any USB or Bluetooth mouse

## Installation

1. Download and unzip `MacScrollFix.app.zip`
2. Move `MacScrollFix.app` to your **Applications** folder
3. **Right-click → Open** (first launch only, to bypass Gatekeeper)
4. Grant **Accessibility** access when prompted
5. The `↕` icon appears in your menu bar — you're done

---

## Settings

Click the menu bar icon to open settings.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| **Enable** | Toggle the app on/off without quitting |
| **Launch at Login** | Start automatically when you log in |
| **Smoothness** | Off / Regular / High — controls momentum decay |
| **Speed** | Slow / Medium / Fast — controls scroll velocity |
| **Reverse Direction** | Flip mouse scroll direction independently of trackpad |
| **Precision** | Moving the wheel slowly scrolls line by line |
